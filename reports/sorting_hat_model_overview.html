Overview

What is Sorting Hat?

Every time a contact becomes an MQL, Sorting Hat automatically calculates their probability of becoming a Closed Won deal and writes it back to HubSpot — no manual scoring, no spreadsheets.

One number, instantly. A score of 80 means the model believes this contact has an 80% chance of becoming a customer, based on patterns learned from 2,573 historical deals. A score of 20 means 20% — low priority, nurture sequence.

Score Behavior

How Scores Separate Won from Lost

When we score all historical deals, won and lost deals land in very different places. This separation is what makes the model useful.

Hot leads (44% of MQLs) close at 94% — that's 12.1x higher than Cold leads (8%). BDRs working a Hot lead are starting from a fundamentally different position than those working a Cold one.

Version History

What Changed from v1

Sorting Hat is a ground-up rebuild from raw HubSpot data — using only signals present at MQL time to ensure the score is accurate and available immediately.

v1 — Previous Model

  • Only 11 features
  • No pre-MQL engagement signals
  • No TAM / company fit data
  • No UTM / attribution signals
  • Over-relied on job title (93% of scores)
  • Demo Requests scored too low (25/100)
  • 50% of MQLs scored as COLD
  • Trained on a pre-engineered feature file

Current — Sorting Hat

  • 20 features across 4 categories
  • Pre-MQL email engagement (opens, clicks)
  • Website sessions and page views
  • TAM data: Google reviews, rating, practitioners
  • UTM source, medium, and deal channel
  • AUC 0.899 — validated across 5 held-out folds
  • Trained directly from raw HubSpot exports
  • Runs automatically on every new MQL via webhook
